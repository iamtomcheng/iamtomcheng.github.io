About me

I am a recent PhD graduate in experimental particle physics from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor. (Defended in May 2017)

I am currently working on the correlation measurement of inclusively-produced Lambda hyperon pairs using data taken from the ATLAS detector under the supervision of Professor Homer Neal. I joined the ATLAS group of Michigan in the summer of 2011 when I was a pre-candidate and I have worked on several projects since then. I have been staying at CERN since fall 2012 after I proceeded to candidacy.

Besides my research, I also worked closely with our research scientist on managing the MDT configuration database as well as helped develop a graphical user interface (GUI) for other users to monitor and modify the MDT configurations more conveniently. In addition, I also participated in the commissioning of the newly installed MDT EE chambers of the ATLAS detector in winter 2013 during the first long shutdown.

This site contains information about myself and my research interest. Feel free to contact me if you would like to discuss any topic in detail. I hope you found the information presented here useful and enjoyed your visit here!
